Underwater Animals

Octopus

The octopus is a soft-bodied, eight-armed mollusc of the order Octopoda.

Did you know that octopuses use camouflage when hunting, in order to avoid predators?

Anglerfish

Anglerfish are bony fish named for their characteristic mode of predation, in which a fleshy growth from the fish's head (the esca or illicium) acts as a lure.

Some deep-sea anglerfish emit light from their esca to attract pray.

Goblin Shark

The goblin shark is a rare species of deep-sea shark with an elongated, flattened snout, and highly protrusible jaws containing prominent nail-like teeth.

The goblin shark feeds mainly on teleost fishes such as rattails and dragonfishes.

Sea Lion

Sea lions are sea mammals characterized by external ear flaps, long foreflippers, the ability to walk on all fours, and short, thick hair, big chest and belly.

Sea lions have an average lifespan of 20-30 years.

Manatees

Manatees (family Trichechidae, genus Trichechus) are large, fully aquatic, mostly herbivorous marine mammals sometimes known as sea cows.

Generally, manatees swim at around 5 to 8 kilometers per hour; however, they have been known to swim at up to 30 kilometers per hour in short bursts.

Walrus

The walrus (Odobenus rosmarus) is a large flippered marine mammal with a discontinuous distribution about the North Pole in the Arctic Ocean and subarctic seas of the Northern Hemisphere.

Walruses have vibressae (whiskers) surrounding their tusks, which, like in other mammels, are specialised for tactile sensing.
